Pickleball Museum — Digital Experience
A full website design for a museum dedicated to the history and culture of pickleball — one of the fastest-growing sports in America. The goal was to create an engaging, educational experience that feels welcoming to both long-time fans and total newcomers to the sport.
  • Researched museum website conventions and sports culture audiences
  • Designed an intuitive navigation system for exhibits, history, and visitor info
  • Built a visual identity that balances fun energy with credibility
  • Developed fully responsive layouts for mobile and desktop

About Me
Good design is as little design as necessary — but never less.

I'm Isabella, a UX/UI Design student based in New Jersey. I'm drawn to the intersection of aesthetics and function — the sweet spot where something looks beautiful and actually works.

Currently developing my skills in user research, interface design, and prototyping. I believe in research-first design — understanding real users before touching a single pixel.

My goal is to join a product team where I can help shape digital experiences that are accessible, inclusive, and genuinely delightful to use.

How I Work

My Design Process

Every project follows a structured approach — from research to high-fidelity delivery.

01
Discover
User interviews, competitive analysis, and defining the real problem before jumping to solutions.
02
Define
Synthesizing research into personas, journey maps, and a clear problem statement.
03
Design
Wireframes → design system → high-fidelity UI, with constant feedback loops throughout.
04
Deliver
Interactive prototypes, developer handoff specs, and usability testing to validate the solution.
